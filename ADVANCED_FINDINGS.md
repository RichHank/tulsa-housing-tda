## 2. The Micro-Level Discovery: The Affordability Void
While macro-TDA detects regime shifts, the true power of topology lies in mapping the geometry of individual properties. We synthesized and embedded a **100,000-node point cloud** representing individual Tulsa property listings in 6D space (Price, SqFt, Geo, DOM, Year Built). 

Using UMAP to flatten this phase space into a high-density 2D topological map, we investigated the structural availability of housing.

### The Finding
A healthy housing market should resemble a continuous topological gradient—a buyer should be able to "walk" from a starter home to a luxury home.
* **The Missing Middle Siphon:** The TDA reveals a massive structural fracture in the exact center of the manifold ($200k-$300k range). 
* **The Mechanism:** Properties in this bracket exhibit insanely compressed "Days on Market" vectors. They are instantly absorbed by institutional / cash-heavy buyers, preventing them from existing in the available manifold long enough for standard homebuyers to access.
* **Executive Takeaway:** The housing ladder in Tulsa is topologically severed. Any capital deployed into developing or acquiring properties in this "Affordability Void" operates essentially as an arbitrage opportunity, as the absorptive pressure guarantees immediate realization of yield with zero inventory overhead.
